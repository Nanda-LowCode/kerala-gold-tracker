## Is Kozhikode's Gold Rate Different from Kochi?

No. The Kerala Gold & Silver Merchants Association sets a **single daily rate** that applies uniformly across all districts of Kerala. Whether you're buying gold in Kozhikode, Kochi, Thrissur, Trivandrum, or Kannur — you'll pay the same board rate per gram on any given day.

This is unique to Kerala among Indian states and makes the gold market here highly transparent and competitive.

## Gold in Kozhikode: Local Context

Kozhikode has a rich gold market heritage, shaped by centuries of spice trade and commerce with Arab and Chinese merchants. The city's Jewellery Street (SM Street / Mittai Theruvu area) is one of the most concentrated gold markets in North Kerala, rivalling Thrissur's famous gold hub.

The district has a high gold consumption per capita — consistent with Kerala's status as India's largest per-capita gold consumer. Wedding season (October–December and February–April) sees peak demand in Kozhikode showrooms.

## Kozhikode Gold Market: What's Special

**Zamorin heritage**: Kozhikode was the seat of the Zamorins (rulers) who famously welcomed Vasco da Gama. The city's trading heritage continues through its gold market.

**Designs**: Kozhikode jewellers are known for traditional Malabar-style designs — heavy Manga Malas, traditional kundan work, and Arabic-influenced pieces that reflect the city's historical connections to West Asia. These typically have higher making charges (15–25%) than machine-made pieces.

**Wholesale activity**: Kozhikode has a significant wholesale jewellery trade serving smaller retailers in Malappuram, Wayanad, and Kannur districts. Bulk buyers often negotiate better making charges.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does gold rate change multiple times a day in Kozhikode?**
No. The KGSMA board rate is fixed once per morning and applies for the entire trading day.

**Q: Can I buy gold at a lower rate than the board rate?**
No legitimate jeweller will sell below the board rate (they would lose money on the metal). Any offer claiming to be below board rate is a red flag — check purity carefully.

**Q: Is Kozhikode gold pure?**
All gold sold in registered Kerala showrooms must be BIS hallmarked. Check for the HUID code on the jewellery and verify using the BIS Care App.
