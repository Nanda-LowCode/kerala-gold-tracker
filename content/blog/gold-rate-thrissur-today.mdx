Thrissur is not just the cultural capital of Kerala — it is widely regarded as the **Gold Capital of India**. The city produces an estimated 70–80% of Kerala's entire gold jewellery output and is home to the country's densest concentration of gold manufacturing units, wholesale dealers, and retail showrooms.

## Why Is Thrissur Called the Gold Capital of India?

Thrissur's dominance in gold comes from a confluence of factors:

**Manufacturing heritage**: The Thrissur district — particularly Thrissur town and the nearby Guruvayur–Kunnamkulam belt — has been home to skilled gold artisans for generations. The intricate workmanship of "Thrissur gold" is recognised across India for its quality.

**Wholesale hub**: Thrissur's SN Park Road and Thrissur Round are among the largest gold wholesale markets in South India. Jewellers from across Kerala and neighbouring Tamil Nadu come here to source finished jewellery.

**Institutional presence**: The Kerala State Jewellery Merchants Association, which sets the daily board rate for the entire state, is headquartered in Thrissur.

**Pooram connection**: The Thrissur Pooram, one of India's grandest temple festivals, drives enormous gold demand. Gold jewellery gifted at Pooram season is a major cultural tradition.

## Gold Buying Tips Specific to Thrissur

1. **Wholesale pricing is possible** — if you're buying in bulk (wedding orders), Thrissur's wholesale market at SN Park Road may offer better rates than branded retail chains
2. **Verify hallmarking** — Thrissur is a major manufacturing hub; always check for BIS 916 hallmark on 22K jewellery
3. **Compare making charges** — manufacturing towns tend to have lower making charges than consumption cities like Kochi
4. **Custom jewellery** — Thrissur artisans are known for bespoke traditional Kerala designs; commission work is common and often more economical than finished jewellery
5. **Festival prices** — rates spike significantly around Thrissur Pooram (April–May); buy before the festival season if possible

## Major Gold Markets in Thrissur

- **SN Park Road** — wholesale and semi-wholesale market
- **Thrissur Round (MG Road)** — major showroom district
- **Sakthan Thampuran Nagar** — traditional jewellery shops
- **Guruvayur** — temple town with heavy gold demand; some of Kerala's largest temple jewellery retail happens here

## Thrissur vs. Kochi Gold Prices

The base rate is identical — both follow the Kerala Board Rate. The practical difference is in **making charges** and **selection**:

- Thrissur shops, being closer to manufacturing, often have lower making charges (6–8% vs 8–12% in Kochi)
- Thrissur has a wider selection of traditional Kerala designs and antique-style jewellery
- Kochi showrooms tend to stock more contemporary and diamond-studded designs
